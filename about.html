About me

A timeline of my life!

  • Oct 2018 - Went to the Kalunga Community Together with the Liter of Light NGO I was able to spend a week disconnected from the world at a Quilombola community in Brazil: the Kalungas. Over a hundred years ago, escaped African slaves used to run away from the farms they were being kept to remote places in the heart of Brazil where they wouldn't be found and could live in peace. This escaped slaves formed Quilombola communities all over Brazil, the biggest of them being the Kalungas. Slavery ended, but the communities remained, many of them still lacking some basic infrastructure. So we went there to give out some light, but it turns out that we were the ones touched by the light of those amazing people and their traditions. Hopefully, we could make as much of a difference in their lives as they made in ours.
  • Sept 2018 - Teaching Internship Now I'm a professor (or a teaching intern). I'm helped out in the Dynamical control classes lectured by my advisor at UnB, besides aiding him with tests and exercises I'm also helped out by actually teaching the classes on the subject of space state control! It was a great experience, just makes me more certain of what I want to keep doing.
  • Sept 2018 - Back in Brazil Last September I came back home to finish my masters. I was happy to be able to accomplish so many things since then. In particular, I could submit a paper to 2019 IEEE International Conference on Robotic Computing and I'm currently working on some demos using the physical platforms. Also, I hope to be able to submit another paper soon with some extensions to my results so far :)
  • Mar 2018 - Internship in Switzerland This year I was one of three women awarded a grant by the NCCR Robotics consortium to spend six months doing research in Switzerland. My time there was spent at the Professor's Auke Ijspeert BioRob Laboratory where I could learn a lot and contribute with a Demo at the end of the project.
  • Sept 2017 - CCTA conference This was the first major international conference I was able to both go and do an oral presentation! Not only the conference was on the beautiful Big Island of Hawaii, but also there were so many incredible people doing incredible work there! The experience of going to the panels and talking to the researchers was incredible, and more than that, I receive so much positive feedback, that I went home full of ideas about the future!
  • Mar 2017 - I became an engineer In March 2017 I had my graduation ceremony! I finally became a Control Engineer!
  • Mar 2017 - Masters Studies Begin Once I finished my bachelor's degree I went straight away to my master's! It was finally time to study robotics at the graduate level.
  • Dec 2016 - Final undergraduate project presentation After one year of a lot of hard work, I could finally finish my final undergraduate project entitled Methods for Visual Communication and Cooperative Control Between Humanoid Robots. I was awarded the highest grade by the Examination Board.
  • Jun 2017 - Became a volunteer at the NGO Liter of Light I believe in using my skills to help people. So, I joined the NGO Liter of Light! We put sustainable lights in marginalized communities. It is such a beautiful project, I can only be proud to be part of it!
  • Mar 2017 - Masters Studies Begin Once I finished my bachelor's degree I went straight away to my master's! It was finally time to study robotics at the graduate level.
  • Oct 2016 - First Place at the Latin American Robotics Competion At the LARC 2016, in the beautiful city of Recife, we (the UnBeatables team) had the chance to compete at another Robot football competition! And guess what? We won!
  • Oct 2016 - PIBIC presentation I presented at the Joint 13th Scientific Initiation and 22nd Univ. of Brasilia Congress for undergraduate fellows my scientific initiation project. Many people seem to be very interest on the Treasure hunt game I developed for the NAO robot, so much so that I was awarded an honourable mention at the congress!
  • Aug 2016 - Internship At LabCert Time to work! I started an internship at the Lab. of Testing and Certification of Electro-medical equipment (LABCERT).
  • Jul 2016 - Best player at the DropIn-only category of the Robocup 2016 Together with the Unbeatables team I could travel all the way to Leipzig-Germany to compete at the 2016 RoboCup. The experience was incredible and our many programming nights paid out! We took the best player trophy home!
  • Feb 2016 - My First Paper I went to São Paulo to participate in the I BRAHUR (1st Brazilian Humanoid Robotics Workshop)! There I did an oral presentation of my first paper describing a ROS based framework for the NAO robot in the football scenario. It was a great learning opportunity!
  • Oct 2015 - First Place at the Latin American Robotics Competion At the LARC 2015, in the Uberlandia, the UnBeatables team got another prize! First place for the SPL category.
  • Sept 2015 - Outreach The UnBeatables team became an outreach activity! It took us long enough to realize that the NAO robot was perfect to teach and entertain kids, but in September 2015 we finally moved to this direction. We started our monthly visits to children's hospitals and schools, and it was always a great success.
  • Aug 2015 - RoboCup China and Best player award For my first international robotics event, I travelled all the way to the other side of the world to compete at the RoboCup 2015 in Hefei-China. It was incredible, I learned so much and met so many people. I frankly, I had never seen so many robots together in my life! We played in the Standard(SPL) with the NAO robot, in the Drop-In Only category (we just put one robot in the field, and it should be able to play with other team's robots - we were evaluated by performance). We were very excited and nervous (especially me, working as a student team leader), but had an amazing experience! And we were awarded best player in our category!
  • Oct 2014 - My first Robotics Competition We travelled to São Carlos to participate in our first Latin American Robotics Competition. It was amazing to be part of a robotics competition. I learned so much and slept so little, and yet, It was fantastic! Plus, It was the first time there was a robot football competition in Brazil! We were awarded the first place for the newly invented SPL category there.
  • 2013/2014 - Exchange Year in England I was awarded the Science Without Border Scholarship by CNPQ to spend a whole year living in England and Studying at the University of Brighton! What an incredible experience, I met engineering students and professors from all over the world and participated in many activities. I even did a project on CAN networks.
  • Spetember 2010 - Joined University of Brasilia (UnB) Before even finishing high school I was accepted at UnB for the Mechatronics degree! I was incredibly happy and excited about my future as an engineer. And, really, It was fantastic!
